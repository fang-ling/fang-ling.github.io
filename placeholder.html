Tricks of evaluating limits

1 Taylor's theorem

$$f(x)=\sum_{k=0}\frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}(x-a)^k$$

Example 1: Evaluate $\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sin x}x$.

Solution: Expand $\sin x$ in terms of the first order Taylor series, we have: $$\sin x\approx x$$ Then $$\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sin x}x=\lim_{x\to0}\frac{x}{x}=1$$

2 L'Hospital's Rule

$$\lim_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=\lim_{x\to a}\frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$$

3 Indeterminate form: $1^{\infty}$

Step 1: Rewrite the problem as $e$ to the natural logarithm of your function. Since $a^b=e^{\ln a^b}=e^{b\ln a}$. Step 2: (optional) Use Taylor's theorem to simply the natural logarithm of your function if you can. For example ($a=0$): $$\ln(x+1)\approx x$$

Example 3: Evaluate $\lim_{x\to0}(1-x^2)^\frac1{1-\sqrt{1-x^2}}$.

$$\begin{aligned} \lim_{x\to0}(1-x^2)^\frac1{1-\sqrt{1-x^2}}&=\lim_{x\to0}e^\frac{\ln(1-x^2)}{1-\sqrt{1-x^2}}\\ &=e^{\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\ln((-x^2)+1)}{1-\sqrt{1-x^2}}}\\ &=e^{\lim_{x\to0}\frac{-x^2}{1-\sqrt{1-x^2}}}\\ \text{by L'Hospital's rule, we have:}\\ &=e^{\lim_{x\to0}\frac{-2x}{-\frac12(1-x^2)^{-\frac12}(-2x)}}\\ &=e^{-2} \end{aligned}$$

Example 4: Evaluate $\lim_{n\to\infty}\left(\frac{1+\sqrt[n]3}2\right)^n$. At the point near zero, we have: $a^x\approx 1+x\ln a$. Since: $$a^x\approx \frac{a^0}{0!}x^0+\frac{a^0\ln a}{1!}x^1$$ Which yields: $$\begin{aligned} \lim_{n\to\infty}\left(\frac{1+\sqrt[n]3}2\right)^n&=\lim_{n\to\infty}e^{n\ln\left(\frac{1+\sqrt[n]3}2\right)}\\ &=\exp\left({\lim_{n\to\infty}n\ln\left(\left(\frac{1+\sqrt[n]3}2-1\right)+1\right)}\right)\\ &=\exp\left({\lim_{n\to\infty}n\left(\frac{1+3^\frac1n}2-1\right)}\right)\\ \text{by $a^x\approx x\ln a+1$, we get:}\\ &=\exp\left({\lim_{n\to\infty}n\left(\frac{1+1+\frac{\ln3}n}2-1\right)}\right)\\ &=\exp\left(\frac12\ln3\right)\\ &=e^{\ln\sqrt3}\\ &=\sqrt3 \end{aligned}$$

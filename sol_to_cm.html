  1. For which value(s) of $k$ is $\binom nk$ a maximum, when $n$ is a given positive integer? Prove your answer.

We know that the middle value of $k$ to a binomial coefficient is the largest by intuition. i.e., $k=\lfloor \frac n2\rfloor=\lceil\frac n2\rceil$.

Proof:

We can write $$\binom nk=\frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}=\frac{\Gamma(n+1)}{\Gamma(k+1)\Gamma(n-k+1)}$$ where $\Gamma$ is the Gamma-function and $n!=\Gamma(n+1)$.

We need to prove that $\binom nk\leq\binom n{\frac n2}$, which is $$\frac{\Gamma(n+1)}{\Gamma(k+1)\Gamma(n-k+1)}\leq\frac{\Gamma(n+1)}{\Gamma^2(\frac n2+1)}$$

For this note that we only need to prove: $$\Gamma(k+1)\Gamma(n-k+1)\geq\Gamma^2(\frac n2+1)$$

Rewrite it using the definition of Gamma-function: $\Gamma(z)=\int_0^{\infty}t^{z-1}e^{-t}dt$. Let $0<\delta<\Delta$.

$$(\int_{\delta}^{\Delta}t^{\frac n2}e^{-t}dt)^2=(\int_{\delta}^{\Delta}(t^{\frac {n-k}2}e^{-\frac t2})(t^{\frac k2}e^{-\frac t2})dt)^2$$

By Cauchy-Bunyakovsky-Schwarz Inequality for Definite Integrals: $(\int f(x)g(x)dx)^2\leq\int f^2(x)dx\int g^2(x)dx$, we have:

$$(\int_{\delta}^{\Delta}(t^{\frac {n-k}2}e^{-\frac t2})(t^{\frac k2}e^{-\frac t2})dt)^2\leq(\int_{\delta}^{\Delta}t^{n-k}e^{-t}dt)(\int_{\delta}^{\Delta}t^ke^{-t}dt)$$

Let $\delta\to 0$ and $\Delta\to\infty$, it becomes:

$$\Gamma^2(\frac n2+1)\leq\Gamma(k+1)\Gamma(n-k+1)$$

  1. Prove the hexagon property,

$$\binom{n-1}{k-1}\binom n{k+1}\binom{n+1}k=\binom{n-1}k\binom{n+1}{k+1}\binom n{k-1}$$

The left hand side $L$ of the equation is:

$$\begin{aligned}L&=\binom{n-1}{k-1}\binom n{k+1}\binom{n+1}k\\&=\frac{(n-1)!}{(k-1)!(n-1-k+1)!}\frac{n!}{(k+1)!(n-k-1)!}\frac{(n+1)!}{k!(n+1-k)!}\\&=\frac{(n-1)!n!(n+1)!}{(k-1)!(n-k)!(k+1)!(n-k-1)!k!(n-k+1)!}\end{aligned}$$

The right hand side $R$ of the equation is:

$$\begin{aligned}R&=\binom{n-1}k\binom{n+1}{k+1}\binom n{k-1}\\&=\frac{(n-1)!}{k!(n-1-k)!}\frac{(n+1)!}{(k+1)!(n+1-k-1)!}\frac{n!}{(k-1)!(n-k+1)!}\\&=\frac{(n-1)!(n+1)!n!}{k!(n-k-1)!(k+1)!(n-k)!(k-1)!(n-k+1)!}\end{aligned}$$

We find that $L=R$ by rearranging terms.

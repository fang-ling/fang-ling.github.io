1 Directional derivative and the gradient

  • Property 1: The directional derivative $\frac{df}{ds}$ in any given direction is the scalar projection of $\nabla f$ in that direction.

  • Property 2: The vector $\nabla f$ points in the direction in which $f$ increases most rapidly.
  • Property 3: The length of the vector $\nabla f$ is the maximum rate of increase of $f$.
  • Property 4: The gradient of a function $f(x,y,z)$ at a point $P_0$ is normal to the level surface of $f$ that passes through $P_0$.

Example 1.1: Let $z=(1+xy)^y$, find $dz|_{(1,1)}$.

By definition, $dz=\nabla z\cdot(dx,dy)$.

To find the partial derivative of $z$ with respect to $x$, we can think of $y$ as a constant and differentiate in the usual way:

$$\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}=y(1+xy)^{y-1}y$$

When we regard $x$ as a constant and use the technique of implicit differentiation to differentiate with respect to $y$, we obtain:

$$\ln z=\ln(1+xy)^y=y\ln(1+xy)$$ Then: $$\frac{\partial z}{z}=\partial y\ln(1+xy)+\frac{yx\partial y}{1+xy}$$ That is: $$\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}=\left(\ln(1+xy)+\frac{xy}{1+xy}\right)(1+xy)^y$$

Now we can calculate then final answer: $$dz=\left(y(1+xy)^{y-1}y\right)dx+\left(\left(\ln(1+xy)+\frac{xy}{1+xy}\right)(1+xy)^y\right)dy$$ $$dz|_{(1,1)}=dx+(2\ln2+1)dy$$

2 Total differential

$$\begin{aligned}dz&=\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}dx+\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}dy\\&=\nabla f\cdot(dx,dy)\end{aligned}$$
